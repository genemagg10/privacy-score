How Web Tracking Works

Understanding the technology helps you make informed decisions about your privacy.

1 The Request

When you visit a website, your browser automatically sends basic information like your IP address, browser type, and language. This is necessary for the site to respond correctly - like knowing what language to display.

2 Cookies & Storage

Websites can save small files to your device. First-party cookies help you stay logged in. Third-party cookies from advertisers track you across different sites to build a profile of your interests.

3 Fingerprinting

By combining many small details - your fonts, screen size, graphics card, and browser settings - sites can create a unique ID that follows you even after clearing cookies.
